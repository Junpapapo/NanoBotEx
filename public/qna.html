Frequently Asked Questions & Q&A

Answers and guides to get the most out of your NanoBot AI Assistant.

Q How do I configure Chrome to enable Gemini Nano's built-in AI?

Enabling Chrome's built-in AI (Gemini Nano) API requires the following configuration setup:

🔧 Built-in AI Activation Steps:
  1. Type chrome://flags in the Chrome address bar and hit enter.
  2. Search for prompt, locate Prompt API for Gemini Nano, and switch its value to Enabled.
  3. Search for optimization, locate Enables optimization guide on-device model, and switch its value to Enabled BypassPerfRequirement or Enabled.
  4. Click the [Relaunch] button at the bottom right to restart the Chrome browser.
  5. Finally, navigate to chrome://components, find Optimization Guide On Device Model, and click [Check for update]. Wait for the AI model download to complete (100%).
🤖 NanoBot: "Think of it as a small brain transplant for your Chrome browser! Just follow the steps, and I'll wake up in no time."
Q Does NanoBot transmit my conversation logs or usage data to external servers?

No, NanoBot operates strictly on-device, prioritizing your privacy.

We do not capture, log, or send your chat records, chatbot names, or customized prompt guidelines to cloud backends. Everything is isolated locally in your browser's private database (IndexedDB / LocalStorage).

🤖 NanoBot: "Your secrets are locked in my local sandbox. Even if the internet goes down, I'll still be standing offline by your side!"
Q What is the Premium AI Buddy feature? Premium

AI Buddies are specialized chatbot companions with custom personas and custom directives.

Beyond the generic chat assistant, you can create and store multiple distinct Buddies — such as a "Code Review Specialist," a "Language Translation Expert," or a "Copywriting Assistant." Simply select and switch your companion from the list to invoke targeted expertise instantly.

🤖 NanoBot: "summon different versions of me for your specific coding or writing tasks! Just don't let us talk to each other..."
Q How do I change the assistant avatar or name?

Open the NanoBot popup window, and find the configuration card on the left.

  • Use the left and right arrows to cycle through the custom avatar illustrations.
  • Type in your desired chatbot name in the text field (defaults to 'Name') to update it in real-time.
  • Click the Shuffle button to randomly pick a unique card from the avatar selection pool.
🤖 NanoBot: "Feel free to change my face or name. Just call me anything but late for lunch!"
Q How can I verify if my local AI engine is successfully connected and running?

Click the [AI ON] badge in the top-right header of the popup window. NanoBot will diagnose the connection to Chrome's underlying model instantly:

  • TEST OK: The local model is loaded and ready to process inputs.
  • TEST FAIL: The background flag configs or model components might be missing or still downloading. Double-check the environment settings guide above.
🤖 NanoBot: "Think of it as clicking my tickle button. If it turns green, my brain is firing on all cylinders!"
